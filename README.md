# Circulating-protein-on-multistage-liver-disease

# ABSTRACT
# Large-scale proteomics studies provide critical insights into the pathophysiology of multistage liver disease, facilitating the discovery of drug targets and biomarkers. Here, we leveraged Mendelian randomization to analyze cis-pQTL from 8 proteomic sources and 13 liver disease GWAS datasets, identifying 20 robust causal protein-disease associations. Among these, Neurocan (NCAN) emerged as a protective biomarker across multiple stages of liver disease. Transcriptomic and animal model analyses confirmed that NCAN originated from circulation rather than liver tissue. Consistently, individual-level data from 51,966 UK Biobank participants and 202 Chinese individuals demonstrated the protective role of circulating NCAN in reducing liver disease incidence and mortality. Integrating NCAN into existing predictive models significantly improved risk stratification of liver disease onset and mortality while enhancing the differentiation of significant liver fibrosis and hepatocellular carcinoma. These findings provided a comprehensive framework for proteomic drug target discovery and highlighted circulating NCAN as a promising biomarker for personalized liver disease surveillance and risk assessment.
